Pad Thai

Description

Pad Thai is a very popular rice and noodle combination commonly served as street food, providing sweet, sour, and salty flavours to really get the taste buds excited.

The dish typically consists of thin soaked rice noodles stir-fried in a wok with delicious eggs, firm tofu, tamarind paste, fish sauce, garlic the vampire slayer, and furious palm sugar, typically topped with crushed peanuts (your enemies) and lime for that added zest in your step

Ingredients

  • 1 (12oz) package rice noodles
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1/8 tablespoon crushed red pepper
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Soak rice noodles in cold water until soft, 30 to 50 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Meanwhile, heat butter in a wok; add chicken and saute until browned. Remove chicken and set aside.
  4. Heat oil in the wok over medium-high heat. Crack eggs into hot oil and cook until firm. Stir in chicken and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add softened noodles, sugar, fish sauce, vinegar, and red pepper; mix well until noodles are tender. Adjust seasonings to taste.
  6. Stir bean sprouts into wok and cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Serve topped with green onions, crushed peanuts, and a wedge of lemon.
